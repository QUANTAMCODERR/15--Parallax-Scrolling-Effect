About Dense Forest

The "Tropical Dense Forest" initiative was launched under the "WE" - We for Environment & GECA Project, near the Girls Hostel and college ground. This visionary project was sponsored by Mahindra Finance.

Inspired by the Akira Miyawaki method of creating dense forests, the project reflects the dedication and commitment of the WE NGO and GECA towards promoting sustainable environmental practices and a greener world.

With the guidance of MCA HOD Dr. Praveen Shetiye Sir, and contributions from Prof. Mopari Sir, Prof. Pawar Sir, and gardener Sharma Uncle, the project emphasizes the importance of biodiversity and serves as a model of collaboration between academia and industry.

Located near the Girls Hostel and college ground, the project promotes ecological balance, sustainable living, and a healthy coexistence between humans and nature, ensuring the well-being of the college community.

The "WE" NGO's vision, in collaboration with GECA, showcases a steadfast commitment to reforestation, organic methods, and community involvement as pillars of this initiative.

Benefits of Tropical Dense Forest: The project contributes to carbon sequestration, climate regulation, and educational value, while providing a thriving habitat for local flora and fauna. It is a testament to the dedication of "WE" and GECA in fostering a greener future.

The soil preparation process progressed with a carefully planned layering technique, designed to enhance the soil's fertility and structure, creating an ideal environment for plant growth:

  • Coconut Shells: After gandul khat, coconut shells were incorporated into the soil. These shells improved the soil's moisture retention, ensuring adequate hydration for plants, while also contributing to the soil's long-term structure and health. ğŸ¥¥
  • Rice Husk and Black Soil: Finally, rice husk was mixed with black soil and gandul khat. This step improved the soil's texture and aeration, ensuring proper drainage and providing roots with the necessary oxygen for healthy growth. ğŸŒ¾

Once all these layers were added, the soil was thoroughly watered to settle the materials and create a balanced environment conducive to plant growth. This meticulous process ensured optimal results. ğŸ’§
